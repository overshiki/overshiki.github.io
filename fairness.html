demographic parity:

Demographic parity requires that a decision be always independent of the protected attributes .

However, as mentioned by Dwork et al, it has two shortcomes:

  1. the notion permits that we accept qualified applicants in the demographic , but unqualified applicants in the demographic , as long as the percentage of acceptance match.
  2. if the target variable is accually dependent on protected attributes, demographic parity will not allow the idea predictor , thus cripples the utils we hope to achieve.

equalized odds:

Equalized odds requires that a decision is independent of the protected attibutes given true labels .

The defination aligns nicely with the central goal of building highly accurate classifiers, since is always a acceptable solution. However, equalized odds enforces that the accuracy is equaly high in all demographics, punishing models that perform well only on the majority.
